    • Language policy

      The use of English in outer circle and extended circle countries is regulated by governments. A country may have an official language (used in administration, news, education) and national languages (example: vernacular). Language policy regulates the power and deployment of an official language, and thereby considers national languages and their importance to the local communities.

    • Code switching

      Code switching refers to the act of switching between two languages/dialects in a single conversation. Code switching serves different functions, including subtle interpersonal functions, stylistic functions, rhetorical functions.

Language encounter

Language encounter occurs at different stages in respect to Kachrus's circles.

Inner circle

Inner circle Englishes are learned at home; learned as native languages.

Outer circle

English has official status in outer circle countries and is learned in educational settings. English is an official language, thus used in official communication, by the govermenment, and news broadcasting.

Expanding circle

English has no official status.

Learning and exposure:

(Formal context) English is learned as a second language at school, English may be the medium of instruction in higher education. English is not generally spoken outside education (exception: Finland).

Time is the enemy of language learning and the effectiveness of adult language education is questionable. Learning English to an advanced level requires commitment and continous exposure to an English enviroment.

(Informal context) English is learned through English talkshows, news channels, TV soaps, YouTube channels, etc. English uses in such channels differ from the formal use in the education settings.

Command of English may be limited (especially spoken English). English may be used in work settings and in online communication with interest groups. Vocabulary limited to particular interests.

Facilitator:

People of a country generally aware of the importance of English: a facilitator of economic success, a requirement for career advancement, access to international research material, globalisation.

Mixed feelings

Advocates of local languages may find exposure to English problematic, may regards English as invasive, undermining local values, and detrimental to the upholding of local traditions.

English may be regarded as liberating and the gateway to the west and western values. Countries with conservative values, may try to actively prevent the spread of English.
