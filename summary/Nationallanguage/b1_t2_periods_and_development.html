Old English

Languages spoken:

(1) English (dialects), Latin (Church), Cornish (Celtic)

(2) Norse (North-Germanic), West Saxon English

English as a product:

Latin-Celtic-Germanic(West,Norse,Gothic)

Events:

(1) Germanic invaders

(2) Invasion by Vikings (North) resulted in Danelaw, divided country into two parts: upper, lower

Language influence and result:

(1) English influenced by
  • West Germanic (present-day German, Dutch, English): English (Foot), German (Fuß), Dutch (foet);
  • North Germanic ‘Norse’ (Norwegian, Danish, Swedish, Icelandic)
  • East Germanic (Gothic)

No unified language. Variety of West Germanic languages (today Germany, Denmark, Holland)

Early Modern

Events: Role of Church and Latin declines Slave trade (1) British colonies (US, AUS, IND) Union of England and Scotland Industrial revolution British Empire Language: English is language of Science and Government Creoles (1): Carribean, Americas Spread: English spreads through colonisation English introduces as medium of instruction (MIO) English becomes language of Advertisement, Consumerism

Languages spoken:

(1) English (Multiple Old English dialects; no standard), French (declining)

English as a product:

Latin-Celtic-Germanic(West,Norse,Gothic)-French

Events:

(1) French lost lands in Normandy

(2) Slave trade, industrial revolution and colonisation (US, AUS, IND)

Change:

(1)

French is abandoned in Grammar schools; English becomes MOI.

Dialects of London and South were gaining prestige (Reason: association with Capital and Court)

Standardisation:

(1) French in decline:

There was need for ‘Standard instruction’:

When dialectal diversity became a concern: decisions based on social and political grounds, not linguistic grounds.

(2) Industrialisation:

Printing became accessible, authors wanted to access an audience as large as possible; a standard form was inevitable;

Government documents (produced by the Chancery) were written in English; it was necessary that they were understood throughout the country; Consistent and uniform language was necessary;

Political: (1707) when Scotland was incorporated into Great Britain, a unified language was necessary. Unity would promote the unity of the country.

(2) Colonisation:

Political: As the British Empire expanded, there was incentive to propagate ‘correct’ English in order to set an example to the rest of the Empire. National identity!
