Variety change, survival, attrition

Time is an important factor in variety formation

Change

Once a variety emerges in a particular form it is continuously exposed to outside threats with the intention to change it. Time-depth (term) refers to the duration of exposure to new circumstances. Example: exposure to French during the Normal-French rule. The longer the exposure the greater the likelyhood of change.

Important: Exposing English to another language is an extremely important factor in the development of a new variety.

Isoglosses -separating dialectal zones- are not static and continously change, with changes stretching over hundreds of years.

If a geographical area is isolated from the rest, the language develops independently and uniquely and with little influence from outside.
Important: a dialect can be innovative in respect to lexis, grammar, and phonology, and develop independently from Standard English.

Survival

Dialect users are responsible for the survival of a dialect; much to it can be attributed to the attitude of speakers towards the dialect, speak identity. A much liked dialect, accepted across a large geographical scope, is more likely to resist supplanting and thus secure survival. Dialect attrition (disappearance) cannot be generalized, as 'Black Country English' in the UK shows. Although a dialect receiving a low rating, it remains popular among younger speakers.

Attrition

Dialect 'attrition' refers to the loss of traditional dialect forms, particularly in urban areas. Reasons for dialect attrition:

  • Migration between dialectal zones erodes dialectal diversity. Distinct dialectal features disappear.
  • Younger language users may refuse to use a dialect ('uncool', not wanting to be identified). Older people remain the only speakers; when they pass, the dialect dies out.
  • If a dialect is not updated (adjusted to present times), lexis may no longer be needed; the dialect becomes obsolete.
  • Identity: if dialect users cannot identity themselves with a dialect (stigma?) they don't use it, and offspring won't learn it. Dies out over time.
