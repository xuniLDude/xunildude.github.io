Political tool

English served as a means of control.

The lack of a common language was exploited by the colonisers as a means of political control.

British colonial language policy was not massively in favour of spreading English.

It is important to understand what decisions colonisers took in respect to the introduction of English into a territory:
Colonial aims English Local language
Compliant and docile population Not suitable for the masses (seen as dangerous) Use it to educate indigenous population
Administration of colonies Elite was taught English
Expression of Political status English regarded as superior.

English embodied the civilised values of the colonising power.

Inequal powers

When English got into contact with other languages it did so within particular relations of power;

Social , economic, and political inequalities privileged ‘English’ and those who spoke it. Such conditions played an important role in shaping new varieties of English.
