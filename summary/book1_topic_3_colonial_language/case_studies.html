Ireland

How Linguistic situation Identity The role of English
English introduced to protect colonists and disadvantage the Irish Colonists adopted part of the Irish language.
Irish gradually vanished due to:
  • Depopulation due to food shortage or emigration (America)
  • Introduction of universal English language education.
Results:
  • Distinct form of English emerged: Hiberno-English, influenced by Irish, learned by monolingual language speakers
  • Today: English is the second 'official' language.
  • Today: English is spoken by most of the population.
  • Personal identities and loyalities changed.
  • Colonists adopted 'manners' and 'fashion' of the Irish.
  • Today: Irish adhere to Irish language for sentimental and indentity reasons.
  • English used as a universal language for education.
  • English represented the Irish population during the independence movement. Catholic church used English too.

America (Displacement)

How Linguistic situation Identity The role of English
Large-scale immigration of English speakers

Mainly immigrants from the South of England

Imported slaves from Africa

Past:

African languages affected English (Early Modern Period).

Influence of indigenous languages on English weak: 'moose', 'racoon', 'Manhatten', 'Massachusetts' are indigenous words.

Settlers keeping close ties with England adopted language changes introduced in England; those that didn't lagged behind the language development. Example: /r/ less pronounciation from England.

Past: Internal differentiation: English dialects developed (English vs. South). Examples:
  • South, { y'all } vs. North, { you }
  • South, { might could fix it}, North, { could fit it }
Other varieties developed:
  • Chicano English
  • African-American (AAE)
  • Italian American
Codification:

American English variety was codified in American English dictionary.

Today 'General American':

A levelled form of English with less differentiating prounounciation exist in America.

 Noah Webster: Codification:

American English variety was codified in American English Dictionary for political reasons and identity shaping

Dictionary for political reasons and identity shaping
  • distinct visual identity from England
  • Use of the dictionary to promoto language and unity; bonding between language and cultural and political identity
  • Distinct cultural indentity for political means
  • A distinct language is an attribute of a independent state.

West Africa (Subjection)

How Linguistic situation Identity The role of English
Population kept in subjection

No substantial settlement by the British, but administered by a few British officials.

Population remained mostly African.

Development:

  • Some Africans received English education.
  • Large number of population used English-based pidgins; also own languages.

Case Cameroon Today:

  • English used as an adminstrative language and for education;
  • English-based pidgin is used in colloquial contexts.
  • A continium of varieties exists between Pidgin and English, used for different purposes;

Uneven levels of intellectuality:

Locals considered of lower intelectual standard; Britain did them a favour (colonisation is a good thing!)

Affection vs dislike:

French-speaking West African colonies has more affection for 'French' (because they were citizens of France); Locals in English-speaking colonies were more on the opposite spectrum;

Controversial!!:

  • English was considered a means to 'modernize' a nation.
  • Others believe that English cannot truly reflect the African experience; this may be due to the harsh colonial experience.

Case { Cameroon }:

  • Locals identity is a product of the affiliation to a colonial language rather than 'ethnicity' (example: Cameroon: English or French)
  • Official languages English and French. Stance towards English is generally good. English is a symbol of group identity.
 English linked with independence. Reasons:
  • Composite: made up of many languages; was considered 'impure' and a very useful 'hybrid' language
  • 'Tribalism' with many languages was not suitable for an independence movement. English was a means for the development of a 'nationalist' identity.
  • English was considered a means to 'modernize' as a nation.

Jamaica (Replacement)

How Linguistic situation Identity The role of English
Replacement of work force from West Africa.

Not only England colonised the Caribbean: England, Portugal, Spain, Dutch

The idea was to bring in slaves with differing language backgrounds to prevent communciation; pidgin was limited to simple tasks.

Development:

Pidgin was a necessary tool used to negotiate between middlemen engaged the slave trade.

Mutiple Pidgins were in use: Portuguese-based, English-based

Today:

Mostly creole-dominant bilinguals (creole and English)

English used in education starts to take on a more standardised form.

Today:

Use of creole firmly linked to a sense of local identity.
