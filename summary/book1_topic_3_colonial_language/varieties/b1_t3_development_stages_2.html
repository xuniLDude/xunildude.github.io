Development stages (5 stages)

There are 5 distinct stages in the formation of an English variety by Edgar Schneiders's model.

Development occurs in 5 stages. Not all colonised territories go through all steps.

Step 1: Foundation

  • Settler bring to the new language to the colonised community
  • Both communities are distinct: indigenous are the owners of the territory, the settlers the representatives of Britain

Step 2: Exonormative stabilization

  • English regulary spoken in the territory. Mainly:
    • administration
    • education
    • legal system
Note: Exonormative referring to the fact that English spoken by settlers asheres to English spoken in Britain (norm giving)

Step 3: Nativisation (MOST DECISIVE

Note: Most important from cultural and linguistic point of view
  • Settlers and territory in general detaches itself from the mother country (Britain).
  • Development of own English variety reflecting cultural practices and linguistic features.

Step 4: Endonormative stabilisation

  • English variety is accepted as a legitimate language in its own rights
  • Variety is actively promoted as important to the culture and identity
  • Variety is fully detached from British English following its own developed norms

Step 5: Differentiation

At this stage the variety is firmly established.

  • Internal variation: varieties developed within the established variety.
  • Different versions form dialects, all based on the base variety.
  • Dialects are formed to suit different applications, regional requirements, and create distinct cultural identities.
