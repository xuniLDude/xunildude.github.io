Roles English plays

Why is Engllish becoming a Global Language? It must be explained based on its special roles it plays.

Role 1 - First language

USA, UK, Australia, NZ

English does not have official status in the UK; Wales, Scotland, Northern Ireland have their own important languages;

Role 2 - Special administrative status

Singapore, India, Nigeria

Role 3 - Teaching policy

  • English is a priority in language teaching
  • For non-Engliuh speaking countries English has priority

Role 4 - Foreign language

American and British English have similar statuses in terms of power; they are both regarded as Standard English.

  • Learning English as a foreign language, for leisure or for monetary reasons
  • Over 100 countries treat English as a foreign language
  • Total countries: 195 => 100/195 => 51 %
  • Mainly Europe, North Africa, Latin America

Role 5 - Power

  • Statistics show that 1/3 speaks English to a useful degree. 2/3 do not speak English.
  • If English is a Global Language is favours those that speak it.

Role 6 - Value association

For First circle countries (Kachru) English has no special connotation.

Speaking a particular accent in two different places may be associated differently (e.g. discrimination, admiration).

In other places, English is associated with international outlook (Japan), economic prestige (Bangladesh)
