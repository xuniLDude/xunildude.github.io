English Family of Languages

Will there be an English family of Languages?

Important: Unintelligibility shall not be the base for a family.

Scenarios

Multi-dialectism:

Multi-dialectism is likely, but could it result in multilingualism?

Fragmentation:

Will English fragment into mutually unintelligible varieties? The past, including Globalization, suggest that due to the new varieties.

Family of languages

What the title means is if there will be English varieties that become mutually unintelligble (by definition languages then). Also, if there will be particular English languages considered languages, not dialects.

  • The need for indentity is always there. In the case of Norway, Sweden, Denmark, different languages, although lots of mutually intelligibility between them. They are considered languages for indentity purposes not because of unintelligibility.
  • Unintelligibility remains a key factor that makes the distinction between a language and a dialect.
  • Establishing a new language is difficult and must satisfy two requirements:
    • 1. the full backing of a community
    • 2. enough political and economical influence to make the decision respected by outsiders
  • Very few examples exist of English varieties turning into 'languages' (one proposal was Ebonics, which was called African-American Vernacular English). It was rejected. Assigning a novel 'name' to a variety of English is very unusual. (African-American Vernacular English is clearly a variety including 'English').

Euro-English

Will there be an official Euro-English?

  • Euro-English: spoken in the European Union (in Brussels).
  • What variety emerges when Germans, French, Greeks speak Englishes affected by their native languages?
  • Features: avoidance of idioms, syllable timing, simplified sentence construction, avoidance of colloquial vocabulary. The intention is not to accomodate 'non-native' speakers, but to have a mutual means to communicate.
