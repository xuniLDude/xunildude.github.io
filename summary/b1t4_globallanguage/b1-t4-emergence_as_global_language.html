Why English became the Global Language

Why did English become a global language?

Intrinsic reasons (Wrong)

General assumptions:

  • English is NOT a world language because it is easy to learn
  • English is NOT an easy language to learn
  • Intrinsic linguistic features make it attractive to learn:
    • No inflections
    • Absence of gender
    • Absence of honorifics (in German 'Sie', 'Ihnen')

Counter arguments:

  • Syntactic, Lexical, Stylistic complexities, and irregularieties in spelling are ignored
  • Languages strongly marked by grammtical gender and inflection (French, Latin) have been international languages

Extrinsic reasons (Correct)

English became a world language for extrinsic reasons. Extrinsic reasons relate to the power of people who speak English (Power).

Colonialisation:

  • English colonies (Kachru's Inner and Outer circles)

Beginnings:

  • UK Industrial revolution (UK)
  • US Population expanded (1700) adding English speakers

Politics:

  • British Empire
  • League of Nations (1920-1946): two official languages: English,French
  • International political gatherings, e.g. ASEAN: English has working role

Economics:

  • (19th century) Britain world leading industrial and trading nation
  • Population expanded significantly. Called the 'workshop' of the world.
  • World Scientists resided in Britain, visiting scientists used English to communicate
  • Banking: (19th century) Britain, USA, Germany. Londing and New York became investment capitals.

Press:

  • Independent press (19th century): Development of printing and independent press mainly in US.
  • Information was censored in Europe, Development of popular news in other languages slow therefore.
  • Establishment of news agengies: Reuters, New York Associated Press
  • Most information transmitted internationally was in English
  • Today, 1/3 of newspaper published in countries where English has special status, majority in English.

Advertising:

  • Billboards, posters, displays
  • American advertising began to travel the world
  • (1970's) most advertising agencies US-owned

Broadcasting:

  • First commercial radio statio in Pittsburgh (US), broadcasting globally (in English)
  • BBC world
  • Voice of America
  • Other countries launched English radio programmes (Italy, Sweden, Germany)

Movies:

  • Past 1915, Hollywood (US)
  • Sound added to motion pictures (==> English)
  • Today, Blockbuster movies released in English (with subtitles)

Music:

  • Jazz, Blues (English)
  • US: Bill Haley, Elvis Presley (English)
  • UK: Rolling Stones, Beatles (English)

International travel and safety:

  • International travel uses English as a working language
  • Flight safety: Airspeak (International Air Traffic Control)
  • Water safety: Seaspeak (Essential English For International Maritime Use)

Education:

  • Science knowledge bases (in English)
  • (since 1960) Higher education uses English as medium of instruction.
  • Also in many countries where English has no official status.
  • English language education is booming

Communications:

  • ARPANET in US (1960s). Countries linking to the net used English to communicate
  • By 2000, 70% of Internet content was in English
  • Amount of non-English content steadily increasing as more non-English native countries publish content.
  • Social networks (US based): Twitter, Facebook, Google
