Describing English

A sociolinguistic approach can be used to studying English that analysis Lexis, Grammar, and Phonology of a dialect or variety.

English varieties can be described based on three areas of analysis:

  • Lexis
  • Grammar
  • Phonology

Sociolinguistics

The study of language and society, or language and social life. Occupation, age and social group affect language use. Speakers use language based on how they want the opposite to identify the speaker.

Social and stylistic stratification: Classification of speakers based on etnicity, wealth, income, social status, and so on. Language users of particular groups use language differently, resulting in stratification.
